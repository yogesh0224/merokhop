Frequently Asked Questions

Find quick answers to your vaccine-related questions.

The recommended vaccines during pregnancy include Tdap, Flu, and COVID-19. They help protect both the mother and baby from infections.

The Tdap vaccine protects against pertussis (whooping cough), which can be severe for newborns. Immunization during pregnancy ensures the baby receives antibodies for protection after birth.

The flu vaccine can be administered at any time during pregnancy, ideally before the start of flu season to maximize protection for both the mother and baby.

Yes, the COVID-19 vaccine is considered safe during pregnancy and is recommended to reduce the risk of severe illness. Consult your healthcare provider for specific guidance.

Yes, live vaccines like MMR (measles, mumps, rubella) and varicella should generally be avoided during pregnancy. Consult with your healthcare provider before getting vaccinated.

Vaccines help produce antibodies in the mother, which are passed to the baby, providing immunity until the baby is old enough to receive vaccinations directly.

Most vaccines, including flu and COVID-19, are safe for breastfeeding mothers. Vaccination can continue to protect both mother and baby from infections.

If you miss a recommended vaccine, consult your healthcare provider to discuss options. Some vaccines can still be administered postpartum to protect both mother and baby.
